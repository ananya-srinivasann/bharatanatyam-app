NRITTA PATH

POSTURES

Here are some tips and tutorials for postures like Natiarambam and Aramandi, plus some common mistakes and how to correct them!

NATIARAMBAM

KEY POINTS:

  • Tricep up
  • Elbow up (Don't sag elbow)
  • Patakam held tightly and up
  • Relax shoulder (don't keep it too high)

THINGS TO AVOID

1. Make sure to keep your ELBOW UP!
2. Make sure to keep your PATAKAM UP/TIGHT!
3. Don't make a BREAK with your wrist! Make sure your Patakam is raised so that your arm line is consistent.

ARAMANDI

KEY POINTS:

  • Knees OPEN (facing the side)
  • Sit VERY DEEPLY (Should be a rhombus-like shape!)
  • Back straight
  • Feet pointing to the sides

THINGS TO AVOID

1. Your knees should be facing OUT to the SIDES!
2. Sit DEEP in Aramandi. Don't stand!
3. Keep your back STRAIGHT! Don't lean forward.

SVASTIKAM

KEY POINTS:

  • Big toe of back foot next to the small toe of front foot!
  • Open feet - front foot should face the side.
  • Heel up for the back leg!
  • Both knees should be open (facing the sides)

THINGS TO AVOID

1. This is TOO FAR apart! Make sure the big toe of the back leg is right behind the little toe of the front leg.
2. The back heel should be held UP and tightly!
3. Both knees should be facing to the SIDES.

PRENKANAM

KEY POINTS:

  • Foot should be stretched, facing the sky.
  • Foot should be placed to the side.
  • Knee should be straight - no bending!
  • Make sure to sit deeply in Aramandi.

THINGS TO AVOID

1. The foot is wrong. It should be stretched tightly, facing the SKY. Here, it's drooping too much.
2. The knee should be STRAIGHT. Not bent!
3. Aramandi should be VERY deep.

MUZHUMANDI

KEY POINTS:

  • Knees should be facing to the sides.
  • Back should be straight.
  • Heels should be lifted.

THINGS TO AVOID

1. The knees should be facing out to the SIDES. Not in front!
2. Make sure the heels are lifted and not on the floor.
3. Back should be straight, don't lean forward!
